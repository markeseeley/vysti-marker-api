As economic downturn continuously impacts Britain in 2012 winter, homeless people became increasingly vulnerable. To contribute toward the lofty cause of tackling poverty, Crisis, a national charity for homeless people, operates the Crisis at Christmas program to offer help to homeless people. In its 2012 charity appeal, Crisis highlights the simple struggle of homeless people and persuades readers, the general UK public, to donate for the Crisis at Christmas program. In the appeal, Crisis reveals the distress of single homeless people, voices the importance of immediate donating actions, and faventes the effectiveness of its Christmas program.

Crisis emphasises the distress and suffer of homeless people in order to develop a sense of sympathy. When describing the miserable situation of single homeless people during Christmas, Crisis describes homeless people as "cold, hungry and alone" to illustrate the extreme physical and spiritual suffer of homeless people during Christmas. Here, the deliberate use of the three words, all of which have strongly negative connotations of poverty and pain, works to evoke a sense of sympathy in the readers, who are affluent, by explaining the actual feelings of being poor in detail, in motivating readers to donate. The distress of homeless people dealt involved by the use of emotional elicition is further reinforced by Crisis' use of contrast in the third lines 2 to 3, where the appeal describes the "pain" poor people incur during Christmas, a holiday where people are supposed to celebrate with their families. Crisis sharply contrasts the common sense of blissful and joy that all everyone is supposed to enjoy, to the actual feeling of "loneliness and desperation of homeless people, as they have 'nothing to enjoy, no one to be with, and little hope of anything better' (line 2). This sharp acute contrast further highlights the immense simple struggle of the homeless people, thereby developing the deep sympathy in potential donors toward the unfortunate poor. Besides homeless people's suffer during Christmas, the long-term distress of poor people is also vividly depicted by Crisis to further emphasise the immense suffer, and thereby making readers more determined to help these unfortunate people. In the second page of the charity appeal, Crisis narrates the lifelong struggle of John, in order to amplify the evoke a sense of sympathy of readers toward poor people. Mentioning

John's tragic experiences, such as his horrifying childhood memories of the death of his mother and younger brother, as well as his suffocating workload, depictions of the ensure to deliver the message that being poor is dire, and such suffer unimaginable to affluent donors serves to establish a menacing tone, which pressures the conscience of affluent readers, making it natural for them to donate and end horrible suffers like as such. Overall, Crisis establishes a strong sense of sympathy toward homeless people, which lays the emotional groundwork for direct appeals call for actions later in the appeal.
